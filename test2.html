1.About

1.1 What Heard Mentality is about

Heard Mentality is an open and unbiased forum for global opinion for history’s and today’s most debated issues.

Heard Mentality sees the internet and the limitless possibilities it presents as the most powerful tool for strengthening the globally important societal virtues of liberty, equality and freedom. People from around the world can now share ideas and experiences like never before in history – creating opportunities never thought before possible.

Heard Mentality strives to fill the role in this new world of interconnectedness and communication as the impartial meeting place on the internet where information and public opinion on important issues can be shared in an open and structured forum.

We recognize that real value is generated by the integrity of our user community and the site – developing policies and mechanisms that promote the creation of user driven knowledge and opinion base while still protecting the individual users’ rights to privacy [link to privacy policy].

All site enhancements and policy development and enforcement will be guided by Heard Mentality’s principle of ‘always do good’. The user never need fear that any other motive than altruism exists. New site features will be launched to facilitate the creation, compilation and the sharing of global free information - in hopes of creating new understandings and wisdom.

1.2 Organization

The Heard Mentality website is operated by The Heard Mentality Foundation - a non-profit foundation with a mission of providing a free, open and unbiased gauge of global public opinion.

Heard Mentality provides the network to support productive and constructive debate and truthful information sharing on global issues.

Heard Mentality has no ownership - simply a dedicated team working together to make this possible. Heard Mentality is made up of its users and all decisions strive to be made with their direct involvement and interests at heart.

3. How Heard Mentality Works

3.1 Points, Rank

You will earn points for coming to and being active on Heard Mentality.

You will earn 1 point for each of the following:

  • 20 Page Views
  • 10 Site Visits
  • 5 Votes
  • 1 Share

When you first create your profile on Heard Mentality you will have the rank of Citizen. As you earn more points, your rank will rise.

Its free to participate in the Resources section.

Why do we do this?

The more agrees a debate or resource post has; the more attention it will get. Similar to how you can only vote once on each issue and have a representative affect on its results. By limiting Agrees and Disagrees, we hope you will be more selective about how you use them.

Everybody has an opinion on everything and sometimes finding concise arguments is like finding a needle in a haystack or a grade 3 class where the teacher has lost control. We want you to be selective on where you voice your opinions in Heard Mentality. We also made it cheaper to agree with what some else already said reducing the clutter that sometimes accumulates in web forums.

3.2 Pundits

Only those users who have been very influential in the debate and on the site will become Pundits. You first become a pundit in one of the sub sections by having other users agree with your debate on the questions within that sections. Being in the top 4 Pundits positions of a section or sub section earns you the right to have unlimited agree, disagree and posting within that section. As your postings become recognized in other sub sections, you may rise to being a pundit of a section and ultimately for the entire site.

4. Guidelines & Principles

Users should take great pride in following these guidelines and principles as a more vibrant social and information network will result. The guidelines and principles have been developed to promote:

  • The integrity of the community
  • The quality of the information collected and made available
  • The quality of the communication

The guidelines and principles provide the framework for creating information and interacting with Heard Mentality as it is different than the other social networks. We also request the help of our community to enforce these policies by flagging those interactions you feel violate the guidelines and principles or by volunteering to be a moderator [How you can help]. Badges [link to badges] will be awarded to those users who abide by and assist in enforcing the guidelines and principles.

In all user interaction:
  • Concise
  • Clean
  • Original
  • Golden Rule
Issues:
  • Concise
  • Clean
  • Original
  • Golden Rule
Posts:
  • Concise
  • Clean
  • Original
  • Golden Rule
Principles:

Concise: Take pity on the reader – information should be presented in a non verbose manner. Pride should be taken from wording things concisely. This will ensure users can consume and retain a larger amount of information as well as making technical features like the translations and search more effective. Studies also show that more people will stop to read a shorter comment and more likely to pass over longer ones.

Clean: No profanity please. Consider all users when choosing your language. People take the posting more seriously when it refrains from using harsh and negative words. Also, abbreviations should be avoided as other users or the translation feature may not understand.

Original: Pleased do not repeat anything that has been already posted. Any duplicate content dilutes the overall accuracy of the compiled information. Any new issue or posting added to Heard Mentality should be significantly different than existing content. Good practice would be to “agree” with the posting you may want to reiterate or voting on the issue that already exists - this will ultimately draw more attention to it.

Golden Rule: This is the fundamental rule to make any social community work. Please treat others like you would like to be treated yourself and you in return will get the same.

Important: Issues should be classifiable into the provided high level sections. Please do not post issues that could be considered unimportant, for example: The hairdo of a certain celebrity or which restaurant or movie is best. While these may be important questions, these are not the types of issues to be discussed here.

Unbiased: Issues should be posted in an unbiased and non-leading way. Issues should be posed simply and direct. All realistic possible answers should be introduced.

On Point: When creating or replying to a debate, please discuss only the issue at hand and reframe from any outside discussions. The Heard Mentality debate works differently than others web forums. We ask you to post meaningful insight or references on the issue and refrain from exchanging pleasantries (this can be done through other communication channels). Recognition of one’s point well made or a good reply to one’s post is encouraged by "agreeing".

Truthful: Never post any false information. Strive to post only what can be backed up by facts. References and links should always be provided when available.

Considerate: Discussions within the debate are about issues and questions surrounding those issues. Postings should never be directed to other users directly, nor should they treat any other user disrespectfully. Please keep personal emotion out of discussion and consider each person’s right to their own view.
